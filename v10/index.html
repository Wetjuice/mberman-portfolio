About

I started modding Unreal Tournament maps at fourteen, and never looked back. What began as geometry and trigger volumes became a lifelong obsession with the invisible architecture of play — the systems beneath systems that shape how people feel, choose, and connect.

Over sixteen years across four studios, I’ve led design on combat systems, narrative frameworks, open-world ecologies, and multiplayer economies. At Phantom Arc I helped define an IP that sold three million copies. At Ironlight Interactive I rebuilt a live-service game’s entire progression loop mid-lifecycle and watched retention double. At Tessera Digital I directed a small team through a critically acclaimed indie launch on a budget that would make most publishers weep.

I believe games are the most powerful empathy machines ever built. Every mechanic is a lens, every system a mirror. When we design well, we don’t just entertain — we give players the vocabulary to understand experiences they’ve never lived. That conviction drives every decision I make at the whiteboard, in the spreadsheet, and in the room.

Now independent, I consult with studios who want design that respects the player’s intelligence and rewards their curiosity. If that sounds like your team, let’s talk.
