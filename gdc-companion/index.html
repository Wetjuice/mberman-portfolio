conversation frameworks
the opener — don't lead with title
❌ "Hi, I'm [name], Game Director at Bend Studio." ✅ "Hey, I really liked your point about [X]. I've been wrestling with something similar — [brief context]." Your title comes up naturally. When it does, it lands harder because they already respect your thinking.
when they ask about bend's cancelled project
"Yeah, it was a tough call but ultimately the right one. The team learned a lot, and what we're building now is stronger for it. Can't say much yet, but I'm genuinely excited about where we're headed." Acknowledge it happened. Frame as learning. Pivot to momentum. Never badmouth Sony, the project, or former colleagues.
when they ask if you're looking
"I'm always interested in what's happening across the industry — that's half the reason to be at GDC. Right now I'm focused on what we're cooking at Bend, but I never close doors." Keeps options open without signaling desperation or disloyalty.
when you want to explore (subtly)
"That sounds like a fascinating challenge. I'd love to hear more about where that project is headed — maybe grab coffee later this week?" Move to 1:1. Never discuss career moves in a group or near your studio colleagues.
for playstation peers
"It's rare we actually get to talk outside of the Sony structure. I'd love to hear how your team handled [specific challenge] — we're navigating something similar at Bend." PS peers are the most valuable connections — allies, collaborators, internal advocates.
the close
"This was great — I'd love to keep this going. Can I grab your email / are you on LinkedIn?" Then immediately note: "Sarah - Naughty Dog - narrative systems." You will forget by Thursday if you don't.
strategic questions for ps peers
1. "How is the internal greenlight process evolving? What does a successful pitch look like now?"
2. "What's the appetite for new IP vs. franchise extension at the studio level?"
3. "How are teams sizing multiplayer scope after the live-service corrections?"
4. "What does Hulst's team look at when evaluating whether a studio stays open?"
hallway conversations — what everyone's talking about
"what's your studio doing about AI?"

Every studio is asked this. Have a thoughtful answer. 52% negative sentiment — frame as empowering creators, not replacing them. 36% adoption rate.

"how are you rebuilding after layoffs?"

Team culture and morale post-cuts is the elephant in every room. 28% of devs laid off in past 2 years.

"is live-service dead?"

No, but the bar just got astronomically higher. 11/19 major 2025 launches lost 80-99% of players. Only top-5 in each genre survive.

"what's sony's next move?"

Their GDC silence fuels speculation. Scaled-back presence — only PS Indie Breakfast confirmed. Strategic silence, not disengagement.

what this means for bend
✅ industry validates bend's return to SP

Every data point — sales, awards, player retention, GDC session focus — points the same direction. Bend's DNA (Syphon Filter → Days Gone) aligns with the market.

🎯 ghost of yōtei is the benchmark

🤖 ai: useful but culturally radioactive

52% negative sentiment rising. Must frame as empowering creators, not replacing them. Your AI-assisted work is ahead of the curve but deploy carefully in studio settings.

📊 job market favours experienced directors

16yr + Game Director + open-world = thinnest talent pyramid. Hiring pool is deepest in a decade. You have leverage whether staying or exploring.

💀 live-service graveyard

Supervive (-99.4%), FragPunk (-98%), most 2025 launches cratered. FBC: Firebreak — beloved SP studio forced into LS with predictable results. Ammunition for any internal debate about project direction.

bend's risk factors
1. the bluepoint precedent

Survived initial cancellation but couldn't get new project greenlit → closed. Bend MUST ensure current project has strong internal support.

2. multiplayer ≠ live-service

Guerrilla's co-op approach (smaller scope, proven IP, not GaaS) is the approved model. Full GaaS is toxic internally.

3. creative director vacancy

Not having a locked-in creative lead is a vulnerability. Arguably the most important hire Bend makes this year.

4. no established franchise for multiplayer

Unlike Guerrilla (Horizon) or Insomniac (Marvel), Bend doesn't have a franchise to build MP around. Days Gone IP status uncertain within Sony.
